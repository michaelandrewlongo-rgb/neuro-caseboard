What determines facial nerve outcome after vestibular schwannoma resection?

SYNTHESISED FROM CORPUS · VERTEX

Facial nerve outcome is governed primarily by tumor size, the nerve's plane relative to the capsule, and the use of continuous intraoperative monitoring. In Koos III–IV tumors the nerve is splayed and thinned over the anterosuperior capsule, where it is most vulnerable to traction during dissection.2 sources

WHERE THIS CAME FROM

Aggregated from two corpus sources that agree — an anatomical description, not a statistic.

RhotonCranial Anatomy & Surgical Approaches · Ch. 9 CPA · p.412
Youmans & WinnNeurological Surgery · Vol. 2 §148 · p.1203

Preserving the arachnoid plane and debulking the tumor interior before capsular dissection reduces traction injury. A proximal stimulation threshold ≤0.05 mA at closure correlates with House-Brackmann I–II at one year. Anterior inferior cerebellar artery branches adherent to the capsule must be preserved to avoid ischemic neuropathy.2 sources

Two grounded claims here. The ≤0.05 mA threshold ↔ HB I–II correlation is one series' result — an average across that cohort, so verify it. AICA preservation is anatomical consensus.

Anatomy at risk

The retrosigmoid corridor exposes the cerebellopontine angle from a posterolateral trajectory. Facial-nerve course over the tumor capsule and the AICA loop within the porus are the dominant determinants of outcome.

WHERE THIS CAME FROM
