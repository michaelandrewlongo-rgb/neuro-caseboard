## Anatomy at Risk
*Structures to identify, preserve, or monitor for this approach.*

- ⚠ The surgical corridor for a pterional craniotomy involves the sylvian fissure, which must be carefully split to access the MCA bifurcation.
  - *Why:* Proper splitting of the sylvian fissure is crucial to safely access the aneurysm while minimizing retraction on the frontal and temporal lobes.

- ⚠ Key landmarks in order include the sphenoid ridge, sylvian fissure, and the MCA bifurcation.
  - *Why:* Identifying these landmarks in sequence ensures accurate navigation to the aneurysm site.

- ⚠ The optic nerve and oculomotor nerve are at risk during a pterional approach and must be preserved.
  - *Why:* Injury to these nerves can result in visual deficits or oculomotor dysfunction, impacting patient quality of life.

- ⚠ The lenticulostriate arteries, which arise from the MCA, must be preserved to prevent ischemic complications.
  - *Why:* Damage to these perforators can lead to significant neurological deficits due to basal ganglia infarction.

- ⚠ The Broca's area is located in the frontal lobe and must be protected during the approach to prevent speech deficits.
  - *Why:* Preserving functional areas is critical to maintaining the patient's language abilities post-operatively.

- ⚠ Anatomic variants such as an accessory MCA or early bifurcation should be anticipated and identified intraoperatively.
  - *Why:* Awareness of variants helps in planning the clip application and avoiding complications.

- ⚠ The anterior clinoid process should not be aggressively drilled unless necessary to avoid injury to the optic nerve.
  - *Why:* Overzealous drilling can lead to optic nerve damage and subsequent vision loss.

## Operative Plan
*Critical steps, decision points, and stop criteria.*

- ⚠ The patient is positioned supine with the head turned to the right and slightly extended to optimize access to the left MCA.
  - *Why:* Proper positioning maximizes surgical exposure while minimizing brain retraction.

- ⚠ A standard pterional incision is made, and the temporalis muscle is reflected to expose the pterion.
  - *Why:* Adequate exposure is necessary to access the sylvian fissure and underlying structures.

- ⚠ Critical steps include opening the sylvian fissure, identifying the MCA bifurcation, and applying the aneurysm clip.
  - *Why:* Each step must be executed precisely to ensure successful aneurysm clipping without complications.

- ⚠ Decision points include determining the need for temporary clipping of the parent vessel to reduce aneurysm tension.
  - *Why:* Temporary clipping can facilitate safer aneurysm manipulation and clipping.

- ⚠ The procedure should be stopped if there is uncontrollable bleeding from the aneurysm dome.
  - *Why:* Recognizing when to halt the procedure can prevent catastrophic hemorrhage.

- ⚠ Closure involves replacing the bone flap and securing it with plates, followed by meticulous dural and skin closure.
  - *Why:* Proper closure is essential to prevent CSF leaks and ensure wound healing.

- ⚠ Intraoperative neurophysiological monitoring of motor and sensory pathways should be utilized to detect potential neural injury.
  - *Why:* Monitoring helps in early detection of neural compromise, allowing for immediate corrective action.

- ⚠ Microsurgical instruments and an operating microscope are essential for precise dissection and clipping.
  - *Why:* These tools are critical for the delicate maneuvers required in aneurysm surgery.

## Risk and Rescue
*Expected and catastrophic complications with rescue sequences.*

- ⚠ Vasospasm is a common complication post-clipping and should be monitored with transcranial Doppler.
  - *Why:* Early detection and management of vasospasm can prevent delayed cerebral ischemia.

- ⚠ Intraoperative rupture of the aneurysm can lead to catastrophic hemorrhage.
  - *Why:* Preparedness for rapid control of bleeding is essential to prevent severe morbidity or mortality.

- ⚠ Preemptive placement of temporary clips on the parent vessel can mitigate the risk of intraoperative rupture.
  - *Why:* This strategy reduces the risk of catastrophic bleeding during aneurysm manipulation.

- ⚠ Immediate application of a temporary clip on the parent vessel is triggered by aneurysm rupture.
  - *Why:* Rapid response to rupture is critical to control bleeding and stabilize the patient.
