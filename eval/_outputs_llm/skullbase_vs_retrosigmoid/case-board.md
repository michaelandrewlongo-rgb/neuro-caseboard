# Case Board — Left 2.5 cm vestibular schwannoma, retrosigmoid resection with hearing preservation

## Anatomy at Risk
*Structures to identify, preserve, or monitor for this approach.*

- ⚠ Confirm retrosigmoid craniotomy with CPA exposure
  - *Why:* Determines sigmoid/transverse sinus relationship, cerebellar retraction, and IAC drilling angle

- ⚠ Identify asterion, sigmoid-transverse junction, emissary vein, endolymphatic sac/operculum
  - *Why:* Landmarks define craniotomy limits and IAC trajectory; anterior sigmoid or low tegmen changes the opening

- ⚠ Neural Structures
  - *Why:* Facial nerve monitoring strategy, hearing-preservation feasibility, and risk of postop deficits depend on tumor-nerve interface
  - [ ] Facial nerve (VII) course and displacement by tumor?
  - [ ] Cochlear nerve (VIII) identified on imaging?
  - [ ] Trigeminal (V) and lower CN (IX-XI) involvement?

- ⚠ Arteries / Perforators / Veins / Sinuses
  - *Why:* Vascular injury is the dominant cause of catastrophic posterior fossa outcome; vein of Labbé/sinus sacrifice threshold depends on venous drainage
  - [ ] AICA loop and relationship to tumor capsule?
  - [ ] PICA, SCA, and labyrinthine artery position?
  - [ ] Sigmoid/transverse sinus dominance and patency?
  - [ ] Superior petrosal vein?

- ⚠ Functional Structures
  - *Why:* Serviceable hearing drives retrosigmoid vs translabyrinthine decision; brainstem compression changes positioning, CSF release, and postop ICU plan
  - [ ] Hearing status (PTA, SDS, ABR if available)?
  - [ ] Is hearing serviceable (AAO-HNS class A/B)?
  - [ ] Brainstem compression and preop swallowing/airway status?

- ⚠ Variants
  - *Why:* Variant anatomy can block IAC access, open mastoid air cells, and alter the safe drilling corridor
  - [ ] High-riding jugular bulb?
  - [ ] Anterior sigmoid sinus?
  - [ ] Dehiscent facial nerve or fundal air cells?
  - [ ] NF2 status?

- ⚠ No-Fly Zones
  - *Why:* Pre-planned residual tumor boundaries avoid brainstem injury, facial nerve sacrifice, and venous infarct
  - [ ] Define dissection limits: fundal IAC beyond transverse crest?
  - [ ] Adherent capsule at brainstem/root entry zone?
  - [ ] Dominant draining veins that must be preserved?

## Operative Plan
*Critical steps, decision points, and stop criteria.*

- ⚠ Positioning
  - *Why:* Suboptimal head turn limits CPA exposure; IONM placement before draping enables early facial nerve mapping
  - [ ] Supine with shoulder roll, head turned 90° contralateral, pins, and slight vertex-down tilt?
  - [ ] IONM setup complete?

- ⚠ Exposure
  - *Why:* Adequate exposure requires skeletonizing the sigmoid sinus; early CSF release relaxes cerebellum
  - [ ] Retrosigmoid craniotomy vs craniectomy?
  - [ ] Drill to expose sigmoid-transverse junction; open dura as C-shaped flap based on sigmoid; CSF release from cisterna magna?

- ⚠ Critical Steps
  - *Why:* IAC opening technique and facial nerve stimulation thresholds define the safety envelope
  - [ ] (1) IAC drilling after dura open — identify fundus and transverse/singular crest; (2) debulk tumor internally; (3) dissect capsule from VII/VIII under stimulation; (4) remove IAC component last?
  - [ ] Planned extent of resection (GTR vs NTR vs STR)?

- ⚠ Decision Points
  - *Why:* Pre-committed decision boundaries prevent escalation-of-commitment and nerve or brainstem injury
  - [ ] When to stop: Stimulation <0.05 mA at brainstem?
  - [ ] Bradycardia from trigemino-cardiac reflex?
  - [ ] Adherent capsule at root entry zone?

- ⚠ Monitoring
  - *Why:* IONM plan should match tumor location and hearing goal
  - [ ] Facial nerve EMG (4 channels, plus free-run)?
  - [ ] BAEP/cochlear nerve monitoring?
  - [ ] SSEP?
  - [ ] Trigeminal motor if large/tentorial tumor?

- ⚠ Facial nerve stimulator, ultrasonic aspirator, endoscope (30°/70°) for IAC fundus, fibrin sealant, dural graft?
  - *Why:* Endoscope identifies residual fundal tumor; fibrin sealant reduces CSF leak risk

- ⚠ Attending Preferences / Questions
  - *Why:* Individual attending practice patterns drive key intraoperative decisions; confirm before incision
  - [ ] Attending preference: Translabyrinthine vs retrosigmoid for this tumor size/hearing?
  - [ ] Preferred IAC closure (muscle plug, bone wax, fascia)?
  - [ ] Postoperative lumbar drain threshold?

- ⚠ Surgery should be stopped if there is a significant drop in ABR wave V amplitude, indicating potential cochlear nerve injury.
  - *Why:* Stopping at this point helps prevent permanent hearing loss, which is a key goal of the procedure.

- ⚠ Closure involves a multilayer watertight dural closure with bone wax application to seal petrous and mastoid air cells, and possibly a fat graft for additional reinforcement.
  - *Why:* A watertight closure is critical to prevent CSF leaks, which can lead to meningitis and other complications.

- ⚠ Obtain an immediate post-operative MRI with DWI, T1+C, and T2/FLAIR sequences to rule out infarct, assess for residual tumor, and check for postoperative hematoma.
  - *Why:* Early imaging provides a baseline, confirms the extent of resection, and allows for rapid detection of ischemic or hemorrhagic complications that may require urgent intervention.

## Risk and Rescue
*Expected and catastrophic complications with rescue sequences.*

- ⚠ CSF leak (incisional, rhinorrhea), facial nerve paresis (temporary vs permanent), hearing loss, headache, cerebellar edema, meningitis, pseudomeningocele
  - *Why:* These are the dominant morbidity drivers; preop counseling and early detection protocols reduce severity

- ⚠ AICA/PICA injury → brainstem/labyrinthine infarct, venous sinus injury → air embolism/venous infarct, tension pneumocephalus, uncal/herniation from cerebellar swelling, CN VII permanent paralysis, death
  - *Why:* Immediate intraoperative recognition and response windows are measured in minutes

- ⚠ Mitigation
  - *Why:* Systematic closure technique is the single strongest defense against CSF leak and infection
  - [ ] Watertight dural closure with graft, muscle/fat plug for IAC, fibrin sealant, layered closure, mastoid air cell waxing
  - [ ] early extubation with neuro exam
  - [ ] CSF diversion threshold

- ⚠ Rescue Triggers
  - *Why:* Each rescue has a 30-60 second recognition-to-action window; the room team needs shared triggers
  - [ ] Sudden bradycardia/hypotension → trigemino-cardiac reflex, release traction
  - [ ] hairline fracture extending to IAM → stop drilling, assess
  - [ ] new VII activity drop → stop dissection, warm irrigation, papaverine
  - [ ] air embolism → flood field, Trendelenburg, left lateral decubitus, aspirate CVP
